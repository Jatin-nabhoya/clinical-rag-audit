## Key Numbers (all verified against real data)

| Metric | Llama-3-8B | Mistral-7B | Phi-3-mini |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Overall correctness | 39.1% | **52.7%** | 36.4% |
| Over-refusal | **54.5%** | 35.5% | 42.7% |
| Fabrication | **0.0%** | 1.8% | 0.9% |
| Gap-filling | 5.5% | 4.5% | **10.0%** |
| Factual drift | 0.9% | 4.5% | **7.3%** |
| Context overlap | **0.567** | 0.483 | 0.199 |
| Answerable correct | 23.3% | **53.3%** | 23.3% |
| Unanswerable correct | **100.0%** | 93.1% | 96.6% |

---

## Three Findings

**1.** Mistral-7B achieves the highest overall correctness (52.7%), driven by the most balanced refusal calibration.

**2.** Over-refusal (35–55%) is the dominant failure mode across all three models — not fabrication (≤ 1.8%). Safety-tuned LLMs in clinical RAG err toward excessive caution.

**3.** Phi-3-mini ignores retrieved context (overlap 0.199 vs ~0.50 for others), completing answers from parametric knowledge — the primary clinical risk in this model.
